Beijing

Beijing

As one of the six ancient cities in China, Beijing has been the heart and soul of politics throughout its long history and consequently there is an unparalleled wealth of discovery to delight and intrigue travelers as they explore the city's ancient past and exciting modern development.



The Highlights

The Great Wall of China

A moment I will never forget... 40 degrees heat, climbing what felt like thousands of knee high steps. The route to the highest point was a struggle, but picking this section of the wall meant there were no other tourists. Beautiful views from the top.

Summer Palace

Summer Palace was sat around a vibrant lake buzzing with tourist from all over China. It is also the largest imperial garden in the world with great symbolic background. The Summer Palace was initially built in 1750 by Emperor Qianglong.

Local Delicacies

Peking duck is a dish from Beijing that has been prepared since the imperial era. The meat is characterized by its thin, crisp skin and is served in several different ways. By the mid-20th century, Peking Duck had become a national symbol of China, favored by tourists and diplomats alike.
